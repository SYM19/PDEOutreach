## Our Story / 我们的故事

It started with a question in mathematics: *can we simulate how a drug moves through a tumor?*

We built **PDEOncology** — a browser-based simulator using reaction-diffusion partial differential equations to model drug penetration in solid tumors. As we developed it, we kept discovering something that surprised us.

Drug resistance and treatment failure aren't always pharmacological. They're often **physical**. Even the most effective chemotherapy drugs can fail to reach the tumor core — blocked by elevated interstitial fluid pressure, dense extracellular matrix, and abnormal blood vessels. And beyond those physical barriers, cancer treatment faces a range of other challenges: late-stage detection, individual genetic variation, and lifestyle factors that silently worsen the tumor microenvironment.

**Even the right drug, given to the right patient, can still fail — because it physically cannot get there.**

This realisation changed what we wanted to build.

We didn't just want a research tool. We wanted people to *feel* how hard this problem is. We wanted a parent, a student, a curious teenager to open a website and understand — without equations, without a biology degree — why cancer is so difficult to treat, and what they can do about it.

So we built **PDEOutreach**.

## What PDEOutreach Does

PDEOutreach takes the science behind PDEOncology and translates it into interactive experiences for everyone:

- **Why Drugs Fail** — simulate real drug penetration with interactive heatmaps. Watch how far a drug actually gets into a tumor.
- **Early Detection** — drag a slider to see how tumor size collapses drug coverage. Feel why "catch it early" isn't just a slogan — it's physics.
- **Lifestyle & Risk** — generate your personal microenvironment profile. See how smoking, BMI, and exercise measurably affect how well drugs would work.
- **Precision Medicine** — compare HER2+ vs HER2− responses side by side. Understand why the same drug works for one patient and not another.

Every section has a **"guess first" quiz** — because we believe the most powerful learning happens when you're surprised by the answer.

---

## Why We Built This

We are two high school students. We did not set out to build a public health platform. We set out to solve a maths problem.

But the more we learned about the physics of cancer treatment, the more we felt a responsibility to share it. Cancer affects almost every family. And yet most people — including patients themselves — don't understand the physical barriers that make treatment so hard.

We believe that understanding cancer at this level changes behaviour. It changes when people go for screening. It changes how seriously they take lifestyle risk factors. It changes how they support people going through treatment.

**PDEOutreach is our attempt to bridge the gap between research and real life.**

## Who We're Trying to Help

We built the bilingual version of both websites specifically to reach:

- **Students in China** — who may not have access to interactive science education tools
- **Developing countries and regions** — where cancer literacy and early detection awareness is lower
- **Families affected by cancer** — who want to understand what is actually happening, not just receive a diagnosis

All our tools run entirely in the browser — no installation, no account, no cost. Forever.
